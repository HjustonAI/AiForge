# Analysis: Why Your Veo 3 Deep Research Prompt Didn't Work

## Executive Summary

The prompt generated by Claude Cowork for Gemini Deep Research suffered from **prompt overload** — the single most documented cause of Deep Research failures. The prompt packed 7+ major research dimensions with 30+ sub-questions into one request, violating the core design principle of Gemini Deep Research: **one primary objective per prompt**.

---

## How Gemini Deep Research Actually Works

Understanding the failure requires understanding the mechanism. Gemini Deep Research is not a simple chat model — it's an **agentic research pipeline** that operates in distinct phases:

1. **Planning Phase**: Gemini reads your prompt and generates a structured research plan, breaking it into sub-tasks. It presents this plan to you for review and editing.
2. **Execution Phase**: The agent browses up to hundreds of websites, reads content, identifies knowledge gaps, and iteratively searches again. It decides which sub-tasks can run in parallel vs. sequentially.
3. **Synthesis Phase**: It compiles findings into a multi-page report.

The reasoning core currently uses **Gemini 3 Pro**, optimized for factual accuracy and hallucination reduction. There is a **60-minute maximum research time** (most tasks complete within 20 minutes), and monthly usage limits apply (5 reports/month for free users, 20 for Pro).

---

## What Went Wrong: Root Cause Analysis

### Problem 1: Massive Prompt Overload (Critical)

The generated prompt contained approximately **7 major research areas**, each with **4–6 sub-questions**, totaling 30+ distinct research objectives in a single prompt. Google's own guidance explicitly warns:

> "Focus on one primary objective or break multiple questions into separate parts. Overloading the first prompt often leads to confusion or output that misses your core needs."

When Deep Research receives an overloaded prompt, it creates a research plan with too many branches. This leads to:

- **Shallow coverage** across all topics instead of deep coverage on any one
- **Research timeout** (hitting the 60-minute limit before completing all branches)
- **Context window overflow** as too many web pages are loaded simultaneously
- **"Research unsuccessful"** or stuck states that many users report

### Problem 2: No Single Clear Objective

The prompt asked Deep Research to simultaneously investigate:

- Model history and origins
- Technical specifications
- Prompting techniques
- Pricing and access
- Competitive benchmarks
- Safety and ethics
- Community ecosystem

Each of these is a complete research task on its own. Deep Research performs best when it can deeply explore one focused area, not when it has to context-switch across seven unrelated domains.

### Problem 3: No Date/Scope Boundaries

The prompt didn't specify date ranges (e.g., "information from 2024–2026 only"). Without temporal constraints, Deep Research may pull outdated information about Veo 1 and Veo 2, mixing it with current Veo 3 data. Google's guidance emphasizes that adding "tight date ranges" significantly improves accuracy.

### Problem 4: No Output Format Constraints

The prompt told Gemini *what to research* but not *how to present it*. Deep Research performs better when you specify:

- Desired report length (e.g., "5–8 pages")
- Output structure (e.g., "use tables for comparisons, prose for analysis")
- What to exclude (e.g., "exclude marketing blogs, focus on primary sources")

### Problem 5: Cowork Delivery Issue

The previous Cowork session output the prompt as plain conversation text rather than saving it as a file. This meant you couldn't easily copy and paste the entire prompt into Gemini — a usability failure separate from the prompt quality issues.

## What Best Practices Say

The **PTCF Framework** (Persona · Task · Context · Format) is the most reliable method for Gemini Deep Research prompts:

- **Persona**: Assign an expert identity (e.g., "You are a technical analyst specializing in AI video generation")
- **Task**: One clear, unambiguous action verb with a single primary objective
- **Context**: Date ranges, source preferences, what to include/exclude
- **Format**: Specify output structure, length, citation style

Additional best practices from Google's own documentation:

- **Start simple, refine iteratively**: Begin with a focused first prompt, then use follow-up questions to expand
- **Edit the research plan**: Before Deep Research starts executing, review and trim the generated plan to remove irrelevant branches
- **Set guardrails**: "Focus on studies from 2024–2026," "Exclude marketing blogs," "Only peer-reviewed or official sources"
- **Use positive framing**: Tell the model what to do, not what to avoid

---

## Recommended Fix: Split Into Focused Prompts

Instead of one massive prompt, break the research into **4–5 separate Deep Research sessions**, each with a single objective:

### Session 1: Technical Capabilities
"Research the technical specifications of Google Veo 3 as of 2025–2026. Cover: maximum video resolution, frame rate, output duration, supported aspect ratios, native audio generation capabilities, and text/image/video-to-video support. Use only official Google DeepMind sources and major tech publications (The Verge, TechCrunch, Ars Technica). Present findings in a structured table format with source citations."

### Session 2: Prompting & Usage Guide
"Research best practices for writing effective prompts for Google Veo 3 as of 2025–2026. Cover: prompt structure recommendations, style descriptors, shot types, camera motion controls, negative prompting support, and common failure modes to avoid. Include 5–8 example prompts that produce high-quality results. Source from Google's official Veo prompt guide and community resources."

### Session 3: Access, Pricing & Comparisons
"Research where and how users can access Google Veo 3 as of 2025–2026, including pricing tiers, platform availability (Google Flow, Vertex AI, Gemini app, API), and geographic restrictions. Then compare Veo 3's capabilities and pricing against Sora, Kling, Runway Gen-3, and Pika. Present the comparison as a structured table."

### Session 4: Ecosystem & Community
"Research the developer and creator ecosystem around Google Veo 3 as of 2025–2026. Cover: API documentation quality, third-party integrations, community tools and workflows, and notable projects or use cases. Include any known limitations, safety features, and content policies. Focus on developer forums, GitHub, and official documentation."

---

## Key Takeaway

Gemini Deep Research is an autonomous agent, not a chat model. It works best when given **one clear mission** with **defined boundaries**. Think of it like delegating to a research assistant: "Go deep on this one topic" always produces better results than "write me an encyclopedia."
